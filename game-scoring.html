Game & Match Scoring

Game Scoring
  • Scoring: 15, 30, 40, Game.
Set Scoring
  • First to 4 games wins the Fast4 set (e.g. 4-0, 4-1, 4-2).
  • At 3-3, play a 7-point tiebreak (e.g. 4-3).
Match Scoring
  • Best of 3 Fast4 sets.
  • Third set is a 10-point super tiebreaker.
Serve on Lets
  • If serve hits the net and bounces in the correct service box, player serves again.
No-AD Scoring
  • Regular AD scoring applies.
Court End Change
  • Change ends every odd game: 1, 3, 5, ...
Toss
  • Toss before every match.
Tiebreaker
  • Tiebreaker at set score 3-3: First to 7 points, win by 2.
  • Super Tiebreaker at match score 1-1 (best of 3) or 2-2 (best of 5): First to 10 points, win by 2.
  • Serve in Tiebreaker:
    • The next person due to serve starts the tiebreak and serves one point to the deuce side.
    • Following two points are then served by the opponent, starting on the ad side. In doubles, the player on the opposing team due to serve will serve these points.
    • Change ends on points 1, 5, 9, ... (every four points).
General Playing Conditions
Match Day & Scheduling:
  • All singles and doubles match lines must be played on the same day.
  • Team captains must exchange lineups simultaneously before match begin.
  • A team wins with a score of 5-0, 4-1, or 3-2 under ideal conditions.
  • At least three match lines must be played for a team to claim a win.
  • Match lines may be played separately only in case of unfavorable weather and the matches can't be played even on the due date.
  • Captains must propose a date and time after consulting their team members.
  • Once captains agree on a date and time, it is binding and can only be changed if both agree. If one captain requests a change, the other may accept or decline.
  • If no date and time are agreed upon, the match defaults to Sunday evening of that week.
  • The team present at the default date and time gets a BYE.
  • If neither team can play, no points are awarded.
Late Arrivals, Line Calls, and Coaching:
  • All players must arrive at least 15 minutes early and complete warm-ups before the start time.
  • A player unavailable at match start loses toss privilege and must accept the opponent’s decision for that match line.
  • For every 5 minutes a player is late, they forfeit one game (5 mins - 1 game, 10 mins - 2 games, 15 mins - 3 games), for that match line.
  • A player arriving 20 minutes late forfeits that match line.
  • Line calls are the responsibility of the players on court; no interference during play.
  • Any feedback about line calls should be communicated to the core team after the match, which will then be shared with other teams for improvement.
  • Volunteers will be arranged for line calls during playoffs.
  • Coaching is allowed only during breaks; no interference during play.
Replacements:
  • No replacements allowed any time during the tournament.
Match Day Result & Team Standings
  • If both teams win 4-4 match lines, check number of sets won, games won, and divide points accordingly.
  • Team Standings:
    • Number of team wins
    • Number of match wins
    • Number of set wins
    • Percentage of games won (up to two decimal points)
    • Lottery (if necessary)
